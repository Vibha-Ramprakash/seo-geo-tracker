3 · The Diagnoser keyword + question engine

This is where the real wins live. Two gaps in one: the long-tail keywords you almost rank for, and the exact questions people ask AI — scored by intent so you know precisely what to write.

Prompt
From my Search Console data, find every long-tail keyword where I rank between position 8 and 20 — my "striking distance" quick wins. Long-tail is ~70% of all searches and exactly how people talk to AI. Fill the "no answer" questions first — that's where AI has nobody to cite yet.

Before you deploy · in plain English

Wait — what are these files, and where do they go?

Claude hands you three things. Here's what each one actually is and where it lives. None of this touches your design or your content — they're small, safe, standard files.

robots.txt Easy

A tiny text file that tells search engines and AI crawlers which parts of your site they're allowed to read. One file, lives at the very top of your site: yoursite.com/robots.txt. Ours simply says "yes, AI bots like GPTBot, ClaudeBot and PerplexityBot are welcome." Without it, some AI engines skip you completely.

llms.txt Easy

The new "menu" you hand to AI. A plain text file at yoursite.com/llms.txt that tells ChatGPT, Perplexity and friends what your site is about and which pages matter most — so they describe and cite you correctly. It's a brand-new standard that almost no one has yet — which is exactly why it's an easy edge.

Schema (JSON-LD) Easy–Medium

A small snippet of code that labels your content so Google and AI understand it ("this is an FAQ", "this is the answer to X"). It goes inside the <head> of the one page it describes — not site-wide. This is what earns rich results and AI citations.

How to actually deploy it — find your setup

  • WordPress Easy, ~10 min — Use the Yoast or Rank Math SEO plugin: it has a robots.txt editor built in. Drop llms.txt in via a file manager plugin. Paste schema into the page's "custom header" box (or the plugin's schema field). No code.
  • Shopify Medium — robots is editable via robots.txt.liquid in your theme; add schema in theme.liquid or a free schema app. llms.txt needs a quick file upload — hand this one to whoever set up your theme.
  • Webflow / Framer Easy — robots.txt is in Site Settings → SEO. Schema goes in Page Settings → "Custom code / inside <head>." Paste and publish.
  • Wix / Squarespace Medium — Use the "custom code / code injection" area in settings for schema; robots is partly managed for you. A couple of clicks, some limits.
  • Custom or coded site (React, Next, static) Easy for a dev — drop robots.txt and llms.txt in your /public (root) folder; put schema in the page's <head>. This is the one to hand to your developer.

Handing it to your marketing or SEO team? Send them this 👇

Copy-paste brief for your team
Please add three things to our site — all standard, safe, and under 30 minutes total, no design or content changes: 1. Merge the attached robots.txt rules at our root (yoursite.com/robots.txt) — they allow AI search crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot). 2. Add an llms.txt file at our domain root (yoursite.com/llms.txt) with the content I've pasted. 3. Add the FAQ schema (JSON-LD) into the <head> of [paste the page URL]. These are SEO + AI-search visibility files generated for us. Ping me if anything's unclear.

How hard is this really? robots.txt and llms.txt are just uploading two small text files — about as hard as attaching a file to an email. Schema is pasting one snippet into a page's header. If you can edit a page in your website builder, you can do this in an afternoon. If not, the brief above gets it done by anyone technical in half an hour.

An artifact only refreshes when you open it — so schedule a tiny daily task to log one datapoint every morning, even on days you don't look.

Prompt
